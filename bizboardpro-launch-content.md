## Reddit Post #1: r/SideHustle

**Title:** I finally stopped bouncing between projects after 2 years of paralysis. Here's what worked.

**Body:**
```
I've been a side hustler for 5 years. 

In that time, I've started:
- A dropshipping store (made $200, quit)
- A YouTube channel (47 subscribers, quit)
- A freelance writing gig (actually did okay, but got bored)
- A SaaS idea (spent 3 months building, never launched)
- 12 other "businesses" I don't even remember

Sound familiar?

I used to think I had a discipline problem. Or that I was "just creative" and couldn't focus.

The truth? I had a decision problem.

I had no system for deciding what was worth my limited time. So I chased every shiny idea that crossed my mind.

2 months ago, I built myself a simple dashboard. Nothing fancy. Just a place to:

1. Capture ideas quickly (before they slip away)
2. Score them based on time/money/energy required
3. Limit myself to 3 active ventures max
4. Do a weekly 15-minute review

The result? I actually shipped something.

It's a simple tool for people like me — serial starters who never finish. I called it BizBoard Pro.

I'm not here to sell you on it. I'm here because I know there are others like me.

People with notebooks full of ideas and nothing to show for them.

If that's you, try this:

**The 3-Venture Rule**
- You can have unlimited ideas
- But only 3 can be "active" at once
- Everything else goes in a "parking lot"
- Review weekly: what's working, what's not, what to kill

That's it. That's the system.

It sounds too simple. I thought so too.

But simplicity is what finally got me unstuck.

If you try it, let me know how it goes.

And if you want to see the tool I built (again, not selling, just sharing), DM me. Happy to give feedback on your projects too.

We're all figuring this out together.
```

**When to post:** Tuesday or Wednesday, 9-11 AM EST
**Follow-up:** Reply to every comment within 2 hours

## Reddit Post #2: r/Entrepreneur (1 week later)

**Title:** I spent 2 years "building businesses" and made $0. Here's what I was doing wrong.

**Body:**
```
Confession time.

I've been an "entrepreneur" for 2 years.

I've read all the books.
Taken all the courses.
Built landing pages for 15 different ideas.

Total revenue: $0

I wasn't lazy. I wasn't stupid.

I was scattered.

I had a Notion workspace with 47 pages of "business ideas."

Every week, I'd get excited about a new opportunity:
- "AI is hot, I should build an AI tool"
- "No, SaaS is better, recurring revenue"
- "Wait, e-commerce is faster to validate"
- "Actually, content is king, I should start a newsletter"

I was busy. I was learning. I was "networking."

But I wasn't building.

The turning point came when I asked myself one question:

"If I could only work on ONE thing for the next 6 months, what would it be?"

I couldn't answer.

That's when I knew I had a problem.

So I built a system:

1. **Capture everything** — ideas, opportunities, random thoughts
2. **Score ruthlessly** — time to build, potential revenue, my interest level
3. **Limit active projects** — 3 max, everything else waits
4. **Weekly review** — what's working, what's not, what to kill

The result?

I finally launched something. It made $2,000 in month 3.

Not life-changing money. But proof that I could actually finish something.

The lesson?

**Ideas are cheap. Decisions are expensive.**

Every hour you spend on the wrong project is an hour you're not spending on the right one.

Every "quick pivot" is actually months of lost momentum.

If you're stuck in idea paralysis like I was, try this:

Write down your top 10 ideas.

Pick the ONE that:
- You can build in 30 days or less
- Solves a problem you personally have
- Has a clear path to first dollar

Kill the other 9. (For now. They'll wait.)

Build that one thing. Launch it. Learn from it.

Then decide what to do next.

That's the system that finally worked for me.

What's your experience? Are you a "serial starter" too? How did you get unstuck?
```
